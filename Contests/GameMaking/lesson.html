Not every programmer wishes to test his programming skills in a contest, but the experience of stepping outside your programming "comfort zone" is entirely beneficial. Even if you don't win a prize, you'll have thought in new ways and be inspired to have another go. Studying how others solved the problem can also be educational.

This contest is a fun challenge for secondary school students with an interest in coding. It is an opportunity for students to showcase their abilities in designing, creating and ultimately playing video games. Once the contest theme is communicated, students will work in teams of four to make a game. They have about two months to plan, design, code, make the art/multimedia files for the game, and test their game. The final stage is showcasing the games at Sheridan - Oakville campus, where Sheridan professors and judges appointed by our school board will decide who the winners are. Usually, there are a couple of categories in the contest, such as "The Best Code", "The Most Technical", "The Most Innovative", and "The Best Theme". Every game entered in this contest can compete in two of these categories.

Best Use of Code
O Noah You Didn't!
Authors: Ahmed El Ashmawy, Luigi Quattrociocchi, Nate Tan
An online, multiplayer, platforming game. It runs on a browser; you compete against other people who also play in a browser. Create an account and join the game. If the current round is already started you will be a spectator and you can watch the other players playing, but you can only play once the current round ended. You play as Noah. Your goal is to bring as many animals as you can to the arc before the flood comes and the timer runs out. You must compete with other player, who also play as Noahs, to see who can save the most animals. You can steal animals from other players. The player with the most animals saved wins. Fully OOP, client/server, based on the Javascript/HTML technology. The server is using Node.js and Mongo.db to store user data, and the client is using the P5.js library. The game has sound effect, background music, animated sprites, and a very user-friendly menu with instruction on how to play. Technologies/Languages used: Javascript, P5, Node.js, Mongo.db, Socket.io, Express, HTML, CSS. Links: http://oh-noah-you-didnt.herokuapp.com/ or http://ohnoahyoudidnt.com

Bible.io
Authors: Tyler Wong, Keith Fernandes. Mark Bedaywi, Yash Nagpal
An online, multiplayer game consisting of several rotating minigames that you play in a browser against other people accessing the website at the same time. For test purposes, you can open the game (website) in two different browser windows. You play as different characters (Noah, Moses, or Jesus), depending on the minigame. Your goal is to collect as many points as you can, and to place higher than your online opponents. The player with the most cumulative points by the end of the rotation (the end of the cycle of minigames) wins. The game has a fully OOP design. It is a client-server game, and it is based on the Javascript/HTML technology. The server is using Node.js, and the client is using the P5.js library. The game has sound effect and background music. Link: https://bibleio.herokuapp.com/

Most Innovative
Sum It Up!
Authors: Maxime Michet, Om Kapadia, Mohanarajah Thushen
An online, multiplayer, educational game, that can help you test your knowledge on bible passages, see the link above. It runs on a browser, and you are competing with other players who are accessing the website at the same time. For test purposes, you can open the game (website) in two different browser windows. You’ll need to type up a summary of the given passage as accurately as you can. The other players will do the same thing. The judging algorithm will analyze the text entered by the players against the original passage and will assign a score to each player. The player with the best skills and knowledge at summarizing bible passages wins. Fully OOP, client/server based on the Javascript/HTML technology. The server is using Node.js and a Python based AI back-end algorithm that compares the user input to the actual passage. The client is using the P5.js library. Link: https://www.sumitup.tk//

David X Goliath
Authors: Farran Javaid, Nelith Nanayakara
Survival game - You are David, and you are fighting waves of Goliaths. With each level you will have to face an increasing amount of Goliath enemies. David has two stats: health(hp) and spiritual points(sp). Use your sling shot to shoot stones at incoming Goliaths. The more faith (spiritual points) you gain the more effective you are. When your spiritual points bar is full, your divine retribution is activated. When you press spacebar an empowering beam of light will help you defeat all Goliath on the screen. If you are defeated in battle (hp is 0) then, game over.Language/Technologies used: Fully OOP designed game coded in Python/Pygame

Google Classroom - Posting to the Class Stream

If your teacher allows, you can communicate with your class on the Stream page using posts, comments, and replies. Some good notes are available here - Post to the Stream page

  • A post is information or a question you add to the class stream. For example: When do we visit the museum?
  • A comment is a response to a post or another comment. For example: We go next Friday.
  • A reply is a response to someone’s comment that mentions them. For example: +commenter’s name Thanks!

Not all teachers allow posts and comments on the Stream page.​If you don't want to post to the Stream page, you can send a private comment to your teacher on an assignment or question.

To ask a question or to share information with your teacher and classmates, create a post:

  1. On the Stream page, click Share something with your class. Note: If you don’t see Share something with your class, your teacher turned off permissions to post.

  2. Enter what you want to say and click Post.
  3. You can attach items to your post, such as images, files, Google Drive items, YouTube videos, or links.
    • To attach a file click on . Drag files to attach or select them from your computer and click Upload.
    • Click Drive . Click Add.
    • Click Youtube . Enter keywords and click Click Add to add the video or Click URL and click Add.
    • Click Link and enter the URL. Click Add.

You can only delete your own posts. If you delete a post, all the comments associated with it are also deleted. You can't undo this action. Teachers can delete anyone’s post and see all deleted posts. To delete a post On the post, click More and click Delete to confirm.

If your teacher allows, you can comment on other posts and comments. Teachers can delete comments and turn off permissions to comment. Comments are text only—you can’t add pictures, links, or videos. To post a comment simply find the post and in the Add class comment box, enter your comment and click Post. When you reply to a comment, the person you reply to is automatically mentioned. You can only use Reply with comments, not posts. Point to the comment and click Reply The person you are replying to is automatically mentioned in your reply. Enter your reply and click Post .

Google Classroom - Turn In An Assignment

Depending on the assignment, you can turn in a personal doc that your teacher assigned to you, create your own Google Doc, or add files to the assignment. If you need to edit work you turned in, you can unsubmit the assignment. But any assignment turned in or marked done after the due date is recorded as late. Some good notes are available here - Turn In An Assignment

  1. Go to classroom.google.com
  2. Click the class -> Classwork -> the assignment.
  3. To attach an item:
    • Under Your work, click Add or create and then select Google Drive, Link , or File Attach

    • Select the attachment or enter the URL for a link and click Add.
  4. To attach a new doc:
    • Under Your work, click Add or create and then select Docs, Slides , Sheets , or Drawings

    • Click the file and enter your information.
  5. (Optional) To remove an attachment, next to the attachment's name, click Remove .
  6. (Optional) To add a private comment to your teacher, under Private comments, enter your comment and click Post .
  7. Click Turn In and confirm. The status of the assignment changes to Turned in.

For more information on how to Mark an assignment Done, or Unsubmit an Assignment, or Check For Late or Missing Assignments check the Turn In An Assignment page.

Organize your Data!

Many students commonly save important work on the desktop of the computer that they are using. For this course, your assignments files will be saved in your individual Student Roster Folder (aka your R:drive student folder). But, what would you do if the files you stored on the computer go poof?

It is vital that you organize and wisely use the limited network storage - roster drive (aka R: drive) and that you backup your data. In this course, you will be required to backup your data to your memory stick and to your DPCDSB-provided Google Drive.

Please organize your Google Drive folder in the same manner as the Student Roster folder, by creating an individual folder for each unit and keeping your files organized by units.

Google Classroom - Google Meet

Google Meet is meant to be a lightweight video chat service.In today’s classrooms, many teachers and students are using video chat services to connect with classrooms across the globe. Teachers are connecting with teachers, administrators are meeting with parents, and students get to meet other students and professional experts. Video chat has expanded the walls of the classroom and Google Meet helps do this better than Hangouts, even though there are less features included with the service. The maximum number of participants in Google Meet is now limited to 30. To join, all someone needs is a link to the chat or a code to enter.

What Are The Features of Google Meet?

  • All calls are encrypted for security
  • Screen-sharing
  • Video recording built-in
  • Can be added from Google Calendar appointments
  • Your next meeting appears on the meets.google.com page

To access Google Meet click on the Google waffle, or some people like to call it the Google cube, and select Meet.

With Google Meet, you can have video meetings with team members anywhere in the world, at any time–all from a computer or mobile device.

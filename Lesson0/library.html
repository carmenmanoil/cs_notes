  • Criterion-on-Demand : Staff now have easy access to 3,100 educationally relevant feature films and documentary titles.  Some of the titles that immediately stood out as being relevant to curriculum: I am Malala, Romeo & Juliet, Life of Pi, Macbeth, Wuthering Heights, Storming Juno, Dunkirk, Mein Kampf, The Book Thief, Being Canadian, and Bully.  These are just a few! A username and password is not needed to access the platform.  To access Criterion-on-Demand (from a board computer/laptop):
    1. Click on the following link in Chrome: http://criterionondemand.com;
    2. You may have to click Log In at the top of the homepage; you do not need a username & password;
    3. Choose a genre/subject button to display movies in a specific category (i.e. literary adaptions, war, Black History, Biography and animation);
    4. Or use the search bar to enter your own keyword search.
  • OverDrive and Libby enable you to borrow and enjoy free eBooks, audiobooks, and more from the Mississauga Library's digital collection; you will have access to 1000s of titles. Please see the instructions below.  All you need to get started is an Internet connection, a library card, and these three steps:
    1. Find your library
    Find and open your library  digital collection using one of the following:
    • Our new Libby app (recommended for public library users on Android, iOS, or Windows 10)
    • The original OverDrive app (for all other library or school users on Android, Chromebooks, iOS, Fire tablets, or Windows 8/10)
    • OverDrive.com (for any user on a Windows or Mac computer)

    2. Borrow
    Borrow a title from your library or school's digital collection. You'll need a valid library card to complete this step. 
    3. Enjoy
    You can enjoy many titles immediately online, download them for offline use for the duration of your lending period.

  • The Media & Professional (M&P) Library now has access to thousands of feature films from the Board’s new Criterion and Audio Cine Films (ACF) feature film subscriptions. Staff can search over 4500 streaming feature films by title, subject, genre and film ratings. Feature Films are integrated with all other digital streaming videos from our Learn360 and National Film Board collection, in addition to the M&P DVD collection. Select the following “Feature Film” search link to view the entire listing of films in this collection, and also note the left margin Genre, Film Rating, Subject and Language “facet” breakdown of this collection. Other “Feature Film” resources to note are as follows on the M&P Feature Film page:
    • The “Movie Ratings Guide” link that provides a breakdown of the 3 different rating systems that are present within this collection.
    • Feature Film Literary Adaptations web page.
    • Web Links to the individual Criterion and ACF feature film platforms.   
  • Users simply search the M&P Enterprise catalogue for a feature film record(s), and within each record is a web-link titled “Click here for the electronic version” that takes the user directly to the feature film to play. Also, select the record’s Title link to display the full record, which contains more film information that includes an Abstract and Movie Rating.
